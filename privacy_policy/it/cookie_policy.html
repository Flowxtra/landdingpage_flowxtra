COOKIE POLICY

Last updated: November 17, 2024

This Cookie Policy explains how Flowxtra GmbH (“Company,” “we,” “us,” “our”) uses cookies and similar technologies to recognize you when you visit https://flowxtra.com (“Website”). It explains why we use these technologies, the rights you have to control them, and how certain cookies may involve personal information.

In some cases, we may use cookies to collect personal information—or combine cookie data with other information to make it personal.

What are cookies?

Cookies are small data files placed on your computer or mobile device when you visit a website. Website owners use cookies to make their sites function properly, improve efficiency, and provide reporting information.

Cookies set by Flowxtra are “first-party cookies.” Cookies set by third parties are “third-party cookies” and enable additional functionality (e.g., advertising, interactive content, analytics). These third parties can recognize your device across different websites.

Why do we use cookies?

We use first- and third-party cookies for several reasons. Some cookies are essential for technical reasons—we call these “strictly necessary” cookies. Others help us track and target user interests to enhance the experience. Third parties may serve cookies for advertising, analytics, and other purposes (described further below).

If you use Flowxtra's Social Media Manager to publish posts or job ads to platforms such as Meta (Facebook and Instagram), LinkedIn, X/Twitter, Google Business Profiles, Snapchat, TikTok, Discord, WhatsApp Channels, Telegram, or other supported destinations, those platforms may set their own pixels or cookies to measure delivery and performance. These third-party cookies are controlled by the respective platform and are subject to their privacy policies. We only enable these integrations at your request, and you can disable them at any time in the campaign or integration settings.

How can I control cookies?

You can accept or reject cookies using our Cookie Consent Manager, which appears in the notification banner and is available on our Website. The manager lets you choose which cookie categories to accept or reject. Essential cookies cannot be rejected, because they are required for services.

If you reject cookies, you may still use our Website, but some functionality or areas may be limited. You can also adjust browser settings to accept or refuse cookies.

How to withdraw cookie consent

You can withdraw or change your cookie consent at any time. Withdrawing consent is as easy as giving it. You have multiple options:

Option 1: Using the Cookie Preferences Button

Click the "Manage Cookies" or "Cookie Preferences" button located at the bottom-right corner of our Website. This will open the cookie preferences panel where you can:

  • Turn off specific cookie categories (Functional, Analytics, Marketing)
  • Click "Accept Necessary Only" to withdraw consent for all non-essential cookies
  • View which cookies are currently active

Option 2: Clear Your Browser Data

You can delete all cookies stored by Flowxtra by clearing your browser's cookies and site data. Note that this will also remove your consent preferences, and the banner will appear again on your next visit.

Option 3: Contact Us

If you prefer, you can contact us directly to withdraw your cookie consent:

  • Email: privacy@flowxtra.com
  • Subject: "Withdraw Cookie Consent"

Important: Withdrawing consent for essential cookies may affect the functionality of our Website. You can always grant consent again by clicking "Accept Cookies" in the banner.

What about other tracking technologies, like web beacons?

Cookies are not the only way we recognize or track visitors. We may also use technologies such as web beacons (also known as tracking pixels or clear gifs). These tiny graphics files include unique identifiers that let us know when specific pages are visited or when emails containing them are opened. Web beacons help us observe navigation patterns, deliver or communicate with cookies, determine whether you arrived via third-party ads, improve site performance, and measure email campaign success. Because web beacons often rely on cookies, declining cookies may limit their functionality.

Do you use Flash cookies or Local Shared Objects?

Some websites employ “Flash Cookies” (Local Shared Objects or LSOs) to store information about service usage, support fraud prevention, or keep certain operations running. If you prefer not to allow Flash Cookies, adjust Adobe Flash Player settings via the Website Storage Settings Panel or manage them globally through the Global Storage Settings Panel. These tools explain how to delete existing LSOs, prevent new ones from being added without consent, and block third-party Flash Cookies. Restricting LSOs may limit the functionality of Flash applications, including those embedded in our services.

Do you serve targeted advertising?

Third parties may place cookies on your device to deliver advertising through our Website. These partners may use information about your visits to this and other sites to show ads for goods or services that could interest you, and may measure ad effectiveness using cookies or web beacons. Data collected through these technologies does not identify you personally unless you choose to share such details.
