Data Processing Agreement

Last updated: November 17, 2024

1. Scope and Parties

This Data Processing Agreement (“DPA”) supplements every service agreement concluded between Flowxtra GmbH, Wipplingerstraße 20/18, 1010 Vienna, Austria (“Flowxtra” or “Processor”), and the customer that subscribes to Flowxtra’s talent acquisition platform (“Customer” or “Controller”). The DPA governs all processing of personal data performed by Flowxtra on behalf of the Customer within the meaning of Article 28 GDPR and the Austrian Data Protection Act.

2. Definitions

Terms such as “personal data”, “processing”, “data subject”, “controller”, and “processor” have the meanings given in Article 4 GDPR. “Sub-processor” means any third party appointed by Flowxtra to process personal data on behalf of the Customer. “Applicable law” means GDPR, Austrian law, and any national laws that implement or supplement European data protection requirements.

3. Subject Matter, Duration, and Instructions

  1. Flowxtra processes personal data strictly for the purposes described in the underlying service agreement, including the provisioning of ATS functionality, candidate communications, scoring, assessments, HR consulting, reporting, and integrations that the Customer elects to use.
  2. Processing begins with activation of Flowxtra services and ends upon full deletion or return of Customer data at the Controller’s written instruction, unless retention is required by law.
  3. Flowxtra acts solely on documented instructions from the Controller. Instructions may be issued via the platform, ticketing channels, or by written notice. If Flowxtra believes an instruction infringes applicable law, it will promptly inform the Controller.

4. Categories of Data and Data Subjects

Details are outlined in Appendix A. In summary, Flowxtra processes personal data relating to:

  • Applicants and candidates submitting job applications or talent-pool profiles.
  • Customer personnel (e.g., recruiters, hiring managers, HR admins) whose details are required for account provisioning, audit trails, and collaboration.
  • Optional references, referees, or other third parties whose information is entered by the Customer or the data subject.

5. Obligations of the Controller

The Controller agrees to:

  • Ensure a valid legal basis for each processing activity performed through the Flowxtra platform.
  • Provide transparent notices to candidates and employees describing Flowxtra’s involvement as a processor.
  • Configure retention schedules, role-based permissions, and anonymisation settings that align with internal policies.
  • Promptly relay instructions relating to data subject requests, deletion, or export.

6. Obligations of Flowxtra as Processor

Flowxtra shall:

  1. Process personal data only on the Controller’s documented instructions, including when transferring data to third countries.
  2. Ensure all personnel with access to personal data are bound by confidentiality commitments and receive regular privacy training.
  3. Implement the technical and organisational measures (TOMs) summarised in Appendix B. Flowxtra may update TOMs provided that the security level is not degraded.
  4. Assist the Controller by providing tools or reasonable support to fulfil obligations related to data subject requests, security, breach notifications, and DPIAs.
  5. Notify the Controller without undue delay (generally within 48 hours) after becoming aware of any personal data breach affecting the Customer’s data, sharing all information necessary for regulatory notifications and remediation.
  6. Maintain records of processing activities and make them available to supervisory authorities upon request.

Job Distribution and Third-Party Platforms

When the Controller uses Flowxtra's custom-domain or multi-channel distribution features, Flowxtra is instructed to transmit job advertisement content (and, where configured by the Controller, candidate information) to external job boards, social networks, or marketing platforms. Flowxtra also provides Social Media Manager tools that allow the Controller to publish posts and content directly to destinations such as Meta/Facebook, Instagram, LinkedIn, X/Twitter, Google Business Profiles, Snapchat, Discord, WhatsApp, Telegram, TikTok, or other integrated platforms. Flowxtra will only share the data necessary to provide the requested integration and will process it strictly in accordance with the Controller's documented instructions.

  • Flowxtra limits processing to the minimum data required for publication (job title, description, compensation range, location, contact methods, and optional tracking metadata).
  • The Controller is responsible for ensuring that each posting complies with the applicable laws, platform policies, and consent requirements of the destination channel and warrants that all necessary notices and permissions have been obtained.
  • Flowxtra may suspend or refuse distribution that violates legal or platform requirements. Any regulatory inquiries, candidate disputes, or platform penalties arising from the job content remain the Controller’s responsibility.

The Controller must ensure that all postings comply with Flowxtra's Job Ad Quality Guidelines in addition to this DPA.

Electronic Signature Service (E-Signature)

When the Controller uses Flowxtra's E-Signature Service to create, send, or manage employment contracts, offer letters, or other legally binding documents, Flowxtra processes personal data on behalf of the Controller as a processor. This includes:

  • Document Content: The text, terms, and content of contracts and documents created or signed through the Service
  • Signatory Information: Names, email addresses, job titles, and contact information of all parties who sign documents
  • Signature Data: Electronic signatures, initials, and any handwritten signatures captured via touchscreen or stylus
  • Authentication Data: IP addresses, device information, browser details, and timestamps associated with document access and signing
  • Audit Trail Information: Complete records of document creation, sending, viewing, signing, and any modifications or rejections

Controller Responsibilities: The Controller is responsible for:

  • Ensuring a valid legal basis for processing signatory data (typically performance of a contract under Article 6(1)(b) GDPR)
  • Obtaining explicit consent from signatories before sending documents for signature, where required by applicable law
  • Providing transparent notices to signatories about the use of the E-Signature Service and Flowxtra's role as a processor
  • Verifying the identity of signatories when required by law or for high-value transactions
  • Ensuring that documents are legally permitted to be signed electronically in the relevant jurisdiction
  • Complying with applicable data retention laws and internal policies for signed documents

Processor Obligations: Flowxtra shall:

  • Process signatory data only in accordance with the Controller's documented instructions and this DPA
  • Implement appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect the integrity and confidentiality of signed documents and signature data
  • Maintain comprehensive audit trails documenting all signature actions, which are retained in accordance with applicable data retention laws
  • Store signed documents securely and make them accessible to the Controller for the duration of the subscription
  • Assist the Controller in responding to data subject requests related to signed documents (e.g., access, rectification, deletion)
  • Notify the Controller without undue delay of any personal data breach affecting signature data or signed documents

Legal Compliance: The E-Signature Service is designed to comply with applicable electronic signature laws, including eIDAS (EU), ESIGN Act (US), and local regulations. However, the Controller is responsible for ensuring that electronically signed documents are legally valid and enforceable in their specific jurisdiction and for their particular use case. Flowxtra does not provide legal advice regarding the enforceability of electronic signatures.

For detailed terms governing the use of the E-Signature Service, see Section 27A of Flowxtra's Terms of Use.

7. Sub-processors

Flowxtra relies on carefully vetted sub-processors to deliver parts of the service (hosting, analytics, messaging, background infrastructure). The current list is available in Appendix C. Flowxtra will notify the Controller of intended changes at least 30 days in advance via email or the platform. The Controller may object on reasonable data protection grounds. If the parties cannot agree on a mitigation plan, the Controller may terminate the affected service by giving 30 days’ notice.

Flowxtra imposes the same data protection obligations on each sub-processor through written agreements aligned with Article 28 GDPR. Flowxtra remains fully liable for the performance of sub-processors.

8. International Transfers

Where personal data is transferred outside the EEA/UK/Switzerland, Flowxtra relies on a legally recognised transfer mechanism, such as adequacy decisions, EU Standard Contractual Clauses (2021/914), or UK Addenda. Supplementary measures, including encryption and access limitations, are applied where required by the Schrems II ruling. Documentation is available upon request.

9. Data Subject Rights

Flowxtra provides in-product functionality that enables the Controller to search, export, rectify, anonymise, or delete data subject records. Where the Controller cannot self-serve, Flowxtra will, upon request, provide reasonable assistance without undue delay and at no additional cost unless the request is manifestly unfounded or excessive.

10. Security Incidents

Flowxtra maintains an incident response plan, including 24/7 monitoring, logging, and root cause analysis. After notifying the Controller of a breach, Flowxtra will provide regular updates until containment and remediation are complete. The Controller is responsible for notifying supervisory authorities and affected data subjects unless the parties agree otherwise in writing.

11. Audits and Certifications

Flowxtra makes available audit summaries, penetration test reports, and attestations (e.g., ISO/IEC certifications) subject to confidentiality. Upon 30 days’ written notice, the Controller may conduct an audit of Flowxtra’s processing activities once every 12 months, or more frequently when required by supervisory authorities. Audits shall be carried out during normal business hours, avoid disruption, and may involve a mutually agreed independent third party. Each party bears its own costs.

12. Return and Deletion

  1. During the subscription, the Controller can export personal data at any time using self-service exports or APIs.
  2. Within 60 days after termination, Flowxtra will make the final export available upon written request. After the export window closes, Flowxtra will begin secure deletion or pseudonymisation of Customer data from active systems and backups according to defined retention schedules (generally within 90 additional days, except where legal obligations require retention).

13. Liability and Indemnity

Each party is responsible for damages caused by its own breach of this DPA or applicable data protection law. Liability caps and exclusions set out in the main service agreement apply to this DPA. Nothing limits obligations under Article 82 GDPR regarding joint liability toward data subjects.

14. Order of Precedence

In the event of a conflict between this DPA and the service agreement, the DPA prevails for all matters concerning personal data processing. If any provision is invalid or unenforceable, the remainder remains in effect and the parties will replace the invalid provision with one that reflects the original intent as closely as possible.

15. Governing Law and Forum

This DPA is governed by Austrian law. Exclusive place of jurisdiction is Vienna, Austria, to the extent permitted by applicable law.

Appendix A – Details of Processing

Nature and Purpose

Hosting the Applicant Tracking System, processing applications, facilitating candidate communications, providing analytics, integrations, assessments, scheduling, HR consulting, email/SMS notifications, and platform support.

Categories of Personal Data

  • Candidate identifiers: name, pronouns, title, date of birth, nationality, contact details, social profiles.
  • Application content: CVs, cover letters, portfolios, assessments, interview notes, offer information, background-check results (if uploaded by the Controller).
  • Employment data: desired role, salary expectations, notice periods, relocation willingness, job preferences.
  • Customer employee data: names, roles, business contact details, account identifiers, usage logs.
  • Metadata: timestamps, device identifiers, locale, workflow actions, communication logs.

Special Categories

Flowxtra does not intentionally process special-category data. Customers are responsible for ensuring that such data is processed only when necessary and with an appropriate legal basis.

Retention

The Controller defines retention periods via platform settings. Flowxtra offers automation to delete or anonymise candidate profiles when retention expires.

Appendix B – Technical and Organisational Measures (summary)

  • Physical security: ISO-certified data centres with 24/7 guards, CCTV, biometric access, and redundant power/cooling.
  • Network security: Segmented VPCs, WAF, IDS/IPS, DDoS protection, managed firewalls, TLS 1.2+ encryption, HSTS, enforced HTTPS, VPN for administrative access.
  • Application security: Secure SDLC, code reviews, dependency scanning, static/dynamic analysis, annual penetration tests, bug bounty programme.
  • Access control: Role-based access, least privilege, MFA, SSO, just-in-time administrative access, logging and monitoring of privileged actions.
  • Data protection: AES-256 encryption at rest, rotating keys via managed KMS, tokenisation for payment data, secrets management, secure backup procedures.
  • Operational measures: Change management, incident response plan, disaster recovery testing, automated backups stored in geographically separate regions, continuous availability monitoring.
  • Organisational measures: Privacy and security training, onboarding/offboarding checklists, confidentiality agreements, vendor risk assessments, regular policy reviews.
